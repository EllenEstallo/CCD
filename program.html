Our Religious Education Program


Central to the our parish life here at Saint James The Less is our Religious Education Program -- a comprehensive curriculum designed for children established by the Diocese of Metuchen.



Our Religious Education Program aims to deliver a strong foundation of the teachings and traditions of our Catholic faith from various aspects of Catholicism that includes scripture, sacraments, prayer, and moral values.


Its mission is to empower students to live out their faith in their daily lives and become active members of the Catholic church.

With extensive experience and expertise in religious education and in parish ministry, Mrs. Rondinelli is equipped with a strong understanding of Catholic doctrine and teachings, and is experienced in working with diverse age groups and educational levels.

Our Catechists


Having undergone extensive training under the Diocese of Metuchen, our Catechists embrace the vocation to teach the doctrine of the Roman Catholic Church.

Our Catechists are individuals who are dedicated to their faith, to our parish families, and especially to our children, who are fully committed to fostering spiritual growth and lifelong faith formation by promotiong a collaborative and inclusive environment for religious education.

Our Catechists' main role is to nurture the spiritual development of the impressionable minds of our young parishioners. Under the support and supervision of our Director, our Catechists coordinate and facilitate the sacramental preparation programs by organizing faith formation activities, retreats and special events. They regularly communicate with the parents and guardians regarding program updates and student progress.


Through a value-based approach to learning, our Catechists set a formative foundation for our children's spiritual growth so that they can be empowerd to live out their faith and apply moral reasoning to their daily lives.
Our Parents As Partners

At every stage of the Religious Education program, your children need your support.

The exhortation of Vatican II declares you, our dear parents, as primarily and principally responsible for the education of their children, as a commitment you have made at the time of your children’s baptism.


Our Religious Education Program places family worship as the most important part of our curriculum. In this regard, it is important that you set the example of being faithful to the Church by worshipping with your children at a weekend Masses and at other important liturgical celebrations. We encourage you to let them see young and old alike at Mass in community prayer and celebration. Remember that they cannot bring themselves, so you, our dear parents, who are their greatest influence, must show them the way.


Your children specially need you during sacramental preparation where occassionally mandated classes and scheduled meetings require your attendance. Please make your effort to support your child in this important events. The schedule will be communicated in a timely manner both via email and via the events and meetings tab of this website.



You have the full support not only of the Religious Education Program, but of the entire parish community of Saint James The Less.

We all adhere and acknowledge the exhortation of Vatican II and are committed to enhance what you already have provided by ensuring that your children receive the proper formation not only to receive the Holy Sacraments in a timely manner but to live lives fully in faith, hope and love of God our Savior in the most Catholic tradition.
Our Pedagogy
For our dear children to learn the formal prayers, the family life concepts and the tools for spiritual readiness necessary to full commission into the Sacraments of First Reconciliation, First Eucharist and Confirmation, the Religious Education Program here at Saint James The Less Church has designed its pedagogy to be conducted as follows:
  • by committing to strive to model God’s love and grace for our children to grow in dignity, respect and compassion, so that they learn to use faith and reason to explore lifelong questions of purpose, meaning, and love;

  • by encouraging our children to know and develop a personal and intimate relationship with Christ, Our Lord and Savior, along with a commitment to love and be of service to His Church: a lifelong process of living a reality of maturity in our Christian Faith;

  • and, by not memorizing information nor having a checklist of requirements, and rather, by our offering of opportunities to grow in the Christian faith and learn from the richness of Catholic life such as participation in Sunday worship, liturgical rites, spiritual retreats, and community service.
