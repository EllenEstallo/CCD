Lessons By Grade Levels

A short description of the liturgical concepts and the prayers to be learned per grade level are as follows:



1st Grade: The basic instructions of our Catholic faith.
  • "The Sign of the Cross"
  • "The Our Father"
  • "Hail Mary"
  • "Glory Be"
  • "Prayer to Your Guardian Angel"

2nd and 3rd Grades: Preparation and Reception of the Sacraments of Reconciliation and Eucharist.
  • "The Act of Contrition"
  • "The Apostles Creed"





4th and 5th Grades: Continued deeping of the children's knowledge of the Catholic faith.
  • "The Rosary"
  • "The Hail Holy Queen"
  • "The Fatima Prayer"
  • "Prayer To the Holy Spirit"


6th Grade: Studies focused on the Sacred Scriptures, and an introduction to the preparation for the Sacrament of Confirmation.
  • "Memorare"
7th Grade: Confirmation Preparation Year
  • Lessons oriented toward forming a relationship with God
  • Christology -- the study of Christ
  • Lessons on faithful discipleship
  • Responsible participation in the life and mission of the Church
  • Lessons on living one’s faith in the world
  • "St. Michael Prayer"





8th Grade: Confirmation Year
  • Lessons on the Holy Spirit
  • A spiritual retreat
  • Community service



Confirmation completes the curriculum. Beyond Confirmation, students are encouraged to continue their faith journey with the assurance of the prayerful support of the entire parish community of Saint James The Less Church. If your child is interested in any parish program at any point beyond Confirmation, please get in touch with our Director, Mrs. Linda Rondinelli.
Lessons On Safe Environment




One of the most important things we can do as a community is to protect our children by educating them about personal safety. It is our responsibility as adults to protect children in our care from harm.

Until they are old enough to put preventative measures in place themselves and understand the consequences of not staying safe, adults need to have a good grasp on child safety in all its forms.


Teaching children simple strategies about keeping safe can help them build confidence, resilience and empower them to be safe in a variety of situations.


Empowering God's Children


It is in this regard that the Archdiocese of Metuchen mandated an initiative called Empowering God’s Children -- a program of age-appropriate lessons designed for parents to educate their children on being safe, specifically by:
  • informing them their right to be safe,
  • teaching them to establish and recognize boundaries for safety, and
  • empowering them to protect themselves when safe adults are not immediately available — and ultimately be safer from people who might want to harm them.
This program provides you with videos to watch with your children, and two accompanying lessons on the following topics:


Lesson 1: on Physical Boundaries: Safe and Unsafe Touching Rules

In this lesson are simple rules about what to do and how to react when someone’s touch is confusing, scary, or makes the child or young person feel uncomfortable. Young people start to deal with the real risks they face when they are out in the world and on their own, and they begin to learn where to draw boundary lines in relationships.

Lesson 2: Safe Adults, Safe Touches and Special Safe Adults.

In this lesson, children, young people, and their parents establish basic guidelines for working together to make certain which friends and other adults in their environment can be trusted to act safely and in the best interest of each child or young person.
